**Last updated: July 1, 2026**

RacePulse is an independent, free sim racing telemetry overlay tool. This policy explains what happens to data in two separate places: the **RacePulse desktop app** and the **RacePulse website** (racepulse.racing). They are not the same, and they are described separately below.

## The RacePulse desktop app

### No account required

RacePulse does not require an account. There is no sign-up, no login, and no profile. You download the app and run it.

### RacePulse does not sell personal data

RacePulse does not sell, rent, or trade personal data to anyone.

### Racing telemetry is processed locally

RacePulse processes racing telemetry locally on your device, reading it from your sim's Windows shared memory or from localhost UDP. RacePulse **does not upload** racing telemetry, lap times, racing inputs, session recordings, driver names, iRacing IDs, or detailed driving data to RacePulse servers.

### Limited app usage analytics

RacePulse does collect limited app usage analytics to understand whether the app is being used and which supported games need the most development attention. This may include app startup events, app version, operating system, supported game detected, and a randomly generated installation ID. The installation ID is not based on hardware information and is used only to measure app usage.

RacePulse may keep limited server logs for security, abuse prevention, rate limiting, and blocking malicious traffic. RacePulse does not use these analytics to collect racing telemetry.

## The RacePulse website

The RacePulse website (racepulse.racing) is separate from the desktop app. To understand site traffic, downloads, referrers, browser/device information, approximate location, and product interest, the website uses two analytics tools: **GoatCounter** and **Google Analytics**.

### What website analytics may collect

Website analytics may include:

- The page URL you visited
- The referrer (the page or site you arrived from)
- Your browser and operating system
- Approximate location (a general region, not a precise location)
- Device information
- Product interest (which pages and features draw attention)
- Interactions such as download clicks

Website analytics are used only to understand traffic to the website. They are **not** used to collect racing telemetry, and they are never used to sell personal data.

### Cookies and identifiers

**GoatCounter** is used for privacy-focused, aggregate analytics. It does not use tracking cookies.

**Google Analytics** may use cookies or similar identifiers where allowed, or where you have consented.

In regions that require consent for analytics cookies (such as the EU, UK, EEA, and Switzerland), Google Analytics uses consent-aware behavior: it starts in a cookieless, consent-denied mode and only enables full analytics after you accept. Elsewhere, Google Analytics may run full analytics immediately.

For more detail, see the [Analytics &amp; Cookies](/analytics/) notice.

### Third-party services

RacePulse links to third-party services, such as **GitHub** (for downloads) and **Ko-fi** (for optional support). When you follow those links, you interact with those services directly, and they have their own privacy policies that govern how they handle your data.

## Controlling analytics

You can block website analytics using:

- Your browser's privacy settings
- Cookie controls or consent choices
- An ad blocker or content blocker
- Firewall rules

If the analytics consent banner is enabled, Google Analytics only loads after you choose **Accept analytics**. You can change that choice at any time from the [Analytics &amp; Cookies](/analytics/) page.
